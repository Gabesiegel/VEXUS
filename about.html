About VExUS AI

Why VExUS?

We are working to advance VExUS use in critical care, internal medicine, and emergency medicine by leveraging artificial intelligence. Our goal is to make venous congestion assessment more accessible and accurate through the integration of cutting-edge technology with established clinical practices.

Dr. Gabriel Siegel, MD

Dr. Siegel is an Instructor and Fellow in Emergency Medicine at the University of Colorado's School of Medicine. His research focuses on the application of point-of-care ultrasound in emergency settings. He co-authored a study titled "Correlation between the VExUS score and right atrial pressure: a pilot prospective observational study," which found a strong association between VExUS grades and right atrial pressure, suggesting the utility of VExUS in evaluating venous congestion.

Dr. August Longino, MD, MPH

Dr. Longino is affiliated with the University of Colorado and has extensively researched venous congestion assessment. He led a study titled "Prospective Evaluation of Venous Excess Ultrasound for Estimation of Venous Congestion," which concluded that VExUS could enhance diagnostic and therapeutic capabilities at the bedside. Additionally, he co-authored research on the reliability and reproducibility of the VExUS score, indicating that ECG-augmented VExUS is a dependable measure interpretable by clinicians from various specialties.

PUBMED PMC

Dr. Matthew Riscinti, MD

Dr. Riscinti is associated with Denver Health and has collaborated with Dr. Longino on VExUS-related research. He co-authored the study "Prospective Evaluation of Venous Excess Ultrasound for Estimation of Venous Congestion," which supports the use of VExUS in clinical practice. Dr. Riscinti has also shared his expertise on the VExUS exam in educational platforms, contributing to the dissemination of knowledge on this diagnostic tool.
