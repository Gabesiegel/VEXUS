VExUS Image Acquisition Guide

Overview

Why Use VExUS?

  • Non-invasive quantification of venous congestion
  • Real-time hemodynamic insight for immediate assessment
  • Guide therapeutic interventions and monitor treatment response
  • Early detection of organ dysfunction risk

1. IVC Assessment

IVC Diameter Measurement

Technique

Patient Positioning

Supine or semi-recumbent.

Probe Positioning

Place a phased array or curvilinear ultrasound probe in the subxiphoid position, just below the xiphoid process. Angle the probe slightly toward the patient's right shoulder to optimize the view of the IVC entering the right atrium.

Measurement Instructions

  • Obtain a clear longitudinal view of the IVC
  • Measure the IVC diameter 1cm proximal to hepatic vein confluence
  • Assess the IVC's collapsibility index with respiration
  • Record both maximum and minimum diameters

2. Hepatic Vein Assessment

Hepatic Vein - Long Axis View

Long Axis Technique

Patient Positioning

Supine or semi-recumbent.

Probe Positioning

Place the curvilinear probe in the right mid-axillary line at the level of the xiphoid or slightly below, angled towards the patient's head.

Instructions

  • Obtain a clear long-axis view of the hepatic veins
  • Apply pulsed-wave Doppler to assess flow patterns
  • Measure velocities of systolic and diastolic waves
  • Evaluate waveform morphology (triphasic, biphasic, or monophasic)

Hepatic Vein - Short Axis View

Short Axis Technique

Probe Positioning

Rotate the probe 90 degrees counterclockwise from the long-axis position to obtain a short-axis view.

Instructions

  • Obtain clear visualization of hepatic veins in short axis
  • Apply color Doppler to identify flow direction
  • Use pulsed-wave Doppler to assess flow patterns
  • Confirm findings from long-axis view

3. Hepatic Vein - Subxiphoid View

Hepatic Vein - Subxiphoid Approach

Subxiphoid Technique

Patient Positioning

Supine or semi-recumbent.

Probe Positioning

Place the curvilinear probe in the subxiphoid position, angling superiorly and slightly to the patient's right to visualize the hepatic veins draining into the IVC.

Instructions

  • Obtain a subxiphoid view of the hepatic veins entering the IVC
  • Identify middle and left hepatic veins
  • Apply pulsed-wave Doppler to assess flow patterns
  • Document waveform characteristics

Doppler Waveforms

  • Normal (Triphasic): Dominant S wave
  • Abnormal (Biphasic): Diminished or absent S wave
  • Abnormal (Monophasic/Flat): Continuous flow pattern

4. Portal Vein Assessment

Portal Vein - Subxiphoid View

Subxiphoid Technique

Patient Positioning

Supine or semi-recumbent.

Probe Positioning

Place the probe in the subxiphoid region, angling slightly to the patient's right and superiorly. The portal vein is typically visualized as it courses anteriorly and to the right within the liver.

Instructions

  • Obtain a subxiphoid view of the portal vein
  • Apply color Doppler to identify flow direction
  • Use pulsed-wave Doppler to assess flow patterns
  • Measure portal vein pulsatility

Doppler Waveforms

  • Normal: Minimally pulsatile (continuous) waveform
  • Abnormal: Moderately pulsatile waveform
  • Severe: Highly pulsatile/biphasic waveform

Portal Vein - Long Axis View

Long Axis Technique

Patient Positioning

Supine or semi-recumbent.

Probe Positioning

Place the probe in either the right mid-axillary line or the epigastric region, just below the xiphoid process. Angle the probe slightly toward the patient's right shoulder.

Instructions

  • Obtain a clear long-axis view of the portal vein
  • Apply color Doppler to confirm flow direction
  • Use pulsed-wave Doppler to assess flow patterns
  • Calculate portal vein pulsatility fraction

Portal Vein - Short Axis View

Short Axis Technique

Probe Positioning

Rotate the probe 90 degrees counterclockwise from the long-axis position.

Instructions

  • Obtain short-axis view of the portal vein
  • Evaluate flow patterns using Doppler
  • Distinguish portal vein pulsations from hepatic artery
  • Document waveform characteristics

5. Renal Vein Assessment

Renal Vein - Long Axis View

Long Axis Technique

Patient Positioning

Supine or semi-recumbent.

Probe Positioning

Position the probe in a coronal plane over the right kidney, between the mid-axillary and posterior axillary lines, and angle slightly toward the patient's head. This maximizes visualization of an interlobar vein in its long axis.

Instructions

  • Obtain a long-axis view of an interlobar vein
  • Apply pulsed-wave Doppler to evaluate flow patterns
  • Assess for continuous vs. pulsatile flow
  • Document any flow interruptions

Doppler Waveforms

  • Normal: Continuous, minimally pulsatile flow
  • Abnormal: Increasingly pulsatile with interruptions

Renal Vein - Short Axis View

Short Axis Technique

Probe Positioning

Rotate the probe 90 degrees counterclockwise from the long-axis position to obtain a short-axis view.

Instructions

  • Obtain a short-axis view showing both interlobar vein and artery
  • Use color Doppler to distinguish vessels
  • Apply pulsed-wave Doppler to assess venous flow
  • Compare with arterial waveforms
