VExUS Image Acquisition Guide

Overview

Why Use VExUS?

  • Non-invasive quantification of venous congestion
  • Real-time hemodynamic insight for immediate assessment
  • Guide therapeutic interventions and monitor treatment response
  • Early detection of organ dysfunction risk

Clinical Indications

  • Suspected volume overload
  • Heart Failure
  • Hemodynamic AKI
  • Shock states
  • Unexplained hypotension

Required Equipment

  • Ultrasound machine with 2D imaging, color Doppler, and pulsed-wave Doppler
  • Phased array or curvilinear transducer
  • ECG module (recommended)
  • Ultrasound gel and gloves

1. IVC Assessment

IVC Diameter Measurement

Technique

  • Position: Supine or semi-recumbent
  • Place transducer 1-2 cm below xiphoid process
  • Measure maximal IVC diameter 2 cm below RA-IVC junction

IVC Collapsibility Index

Assessment

  • Calculate: (Max-Min)/Max × 100%
  • Normal: >50% collapse with respiration
  • Record during normal respiration

2. Hepatic Vein Assessment

Long Axis View

Technique

  • Position probe in right mid-axillary line
  • Angle towards patient's head
  • Identify hepatic vein-IVC junction

Short Axis View

Assessment

  • Rotate probe 90° from long axis
  • Identify all hepatic vein branches
  • Confirm flow patterns

Subxiphoid Approach

Alternative Window

  • Use when intercostal view limited
  • Angle towards right shoulder
  • Visualize hepatic vein-IVC junction

3. Portal Vein Assessment

Long Axis View

Technique

  • Position in right mid-axillary line
  • Locate main portal vein
  • Assess flow direction and pattern

Short Axis View

Assessment

  • Rotate probe 90° from long axis
  • Measure cross-sectional area
  • Confirm flow characteristics

Subxiphoid View

Alternative Approach

  • Use for limited intercostal access
  • Angle towards liver hilum
  • Assess pulsatility ratio

4. Renal Vein Assessment

Long Axis View

Technique

  • Position between mid-axillary and posterior axillary lines
  • Identify interlobar vessels
  • Assess flow patterns

Short Axis View

Assessment

  • Rotate probe 90° from long axis
  • Differentiate from arterial flow
  • Confirm flow patterns
