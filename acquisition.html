1. IVC Assessment

IVC Diameter Measurement

Technique

  • Patient Position: Supine or semi-recumbent
  • Probe Position: Subxiphoid, angle toward right shoulder
  • Measure 1cm proximal to hepatic vein confluence
  • Record both maximum and minimum diameters

IVC Collapsibility Index

Assessment

  • Calculate: (Max-Min)/Max × 100%
  • Normal: >50% collapse with respiration
  • Record during normal respiration
  • Assess for respiratory variation

VExUS Grading System

Grade IVC Diameter Hepatic Vein Portal Vein Renal Vein Clinical Significance
Grade 0 ≤ 2 cm Normal triphasic Normal flow Continuous flow No congestion
Grade 1 > 2 cm Loss of S wave Mild pulsatility Mild pulsatility Mild congestion
Grade 2 > 2 cm Biphasic Severe pulsatility Severe pulsatility Moderate congestion
Grade 3 > 2 cm Monophasic Severe pulsatility Monophasic/Discontinued Severe congestion

Waveform Patterns

  • Normal: Triphasic with dominant systolic wave (S > D > A)
  • Mild Abnormality: Loss of triphasic pattern, reduced S wave
  • Moderate Abnormality: Biphasic pattern, minimal S wave
  • Severe Abnormality: Monophasic or reversed flow pattern
