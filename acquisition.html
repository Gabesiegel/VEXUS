VExUS Image Acquisition Guide

Overview

Why Use VExUS?

  • Non-invasive quantification of venous congestion
  • Real-time hemodynamic insight for immediate assessment
  • Guide therapeutic interventions and monitor treatment response
  • Early detection of organ dysfunction risk

Clinical Indications

  • Suspected volume overload
  • Heart Failure
  • Hemodynamic AKI
  • Shock states
  • Unexplained hypotension

Required Equipment

  • Ultrasound machine with 2D imaging, color Doppler, and pulsed-wave Doppler
  • Phased array or curvilinear transducer
  • ECG module (recommended)
  • Ultrasound gel and gloves

1. IVC Assessment

IVC Diameter Measurement

Technique

  • Patient Position: Supine or semi-recumbent
  • Probe Position: Subxiphoid, angle toward right shoulder
  • Measure 1cm proximal to hepatic vein confluence
  • Record both maximum and minimum diameters

2. Hepatic Vein Assessment

Long Axis View

Long Axis Technique

  • Place probe in right mid-axillary line
  • Angle towards patient's head
  • Assess flow patterns using Doppler

Short Axis View

Short Axis Technique

  • Rotate probe 90° from long axis
  • Identify hepatic vein branches
  • Confirm Doppler patterns

Subxiphoid View

Subxiphoid Approach

  • Alternative when intercostal view limited
  • Position probe subxiphoid
  • Angle towards right shoulder

3. Portal Vein Assessment

Long Axis View

Long Axis Technique

  • Position in right mid-axillary line
  • Identify main portal vein
  • Assess flow patterns

Short Axis View

Short Axis Technique

  • Rotate probe 90° from long axis
  • Identify portal vein cross-section
  • Assess flow characteristics

Subxiphoid View

Subxiphoid Approach

  • Alternative view for limited access
  • Angle towards liver hilum
  • Assess pulsatility index

4. Renal Vein Assessment

Long Axis View

Long Axis Technique

  • Position between mid-axillary and posterior axillary lines
  • Identify interlobar vessels
  • Assess venous flow patterns

Short Axis View

Short Axis Technique

  • Rotate probe 90° from long axis
  • Differentiate from arterial flow
  • Confirm venous flow patterns
