VExUS Image Acquisition Guide

Overview

Why Use VExUS?

  • Non-invasive quantification of venous congestion
  • Real-time hemodynamic insight for immediate assessment
  • Guide therapeutic interventions and monitor treatment response
  • Early detection of organ dysfunction risk

1. IVC Assessment

IVC Diameter Measurement

Technique

  • Patient Position: Supine or semi-recumbent
  • Probe Position: Subxiphoid, angle toward right shoulder
  • Measure 1cm proximal to hepatic vein confluence
  • Record both maximum and minimum diameters
  • Assess respiratory variation

IVC Collapsibility Index

Assessment

  • Calculate: (Max-Min)/Max Ã— 100%
  • Normal: >50% collapse with respiration
  • Record during normal respiration
  • Document both inspiratory and expiratory measurements

2. Portal Vein Assessment

Portal Vein Flow Pattern

Technique

  • Identify main portal vein
  • Optimize color Doppler angle
  • Place sample volume in main trunk
  • Record spectral Doppler waveform
  • Assess for pulsatility

3. Hepatic Vein Assessment

Hepatic Vein Flow Pattern

Technique

  • Identify right hepatic vein
  • Use color Doppler to optimize angle
  • Place sample volume 2-3cm from IVC junction
  • Record spectral Doppler waveform
  • Document flow pattern classification
