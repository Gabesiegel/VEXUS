VExUS Image Acquisition Guide

Overview

Why Use VExUS?

  • Non-invasive quantification of venous congestion
  • Real-time hemodynamic insight for immediate assessment
  • Guide therapeutic interventions and monitor treatment response
  • Early detection of organ dysfunction risk

1. IVC Assessment

IVC Diameter Measurement

Technique

Patient Positioning

Supine or semi-recumbent.

Probe Positioning

Place a phased array or curvilinear ultrasound probe in the subxiphoid position, just below the xiphoid process. Angle the probe slightly toward the patient's right shoulder to optimize the view of the IVC entering the right atrium.

Measurement Instructions

  • Obtain a clear longitudinal view of the IVC
  • Measure the IVC diameter 1cm proximal to hepatic vein confluence
  • Assess the IVC's collapsibility index with respiration
  • Record both maximum and minimum diameters

2. Hepatic Vein Assessment

Hepatic Vein - Long Axis View

Long Axis Technique

Patient Positioning

Supine or semi-recumbent.

Probe Positioning

Place the curvilinear probe in the right mid-axillary line at the level of the xiphoid or slightly below, angled towards the patient's head.

Instructions

  • Obtain a clear long-axis view of the hepatic veins
  • Apply pulsed-wave Doppler to assess flow patterns
  • Measure velocities of systolic and diastolic waves
  • Evaluate waveform morphology (triphasic, biphasic, or monophasic)

3. Portal Vein Assessment

Portal Vein Measurement

Technique

Patient Positioning

Supine or semi-recumbent.

Probe Positioning

Place the probe in the subxiphoid region, angling slightly to the patient's right and superiorly.

Instructions

  • Obtain a clear view of the portal vein
  • Apply color Doppler to assess flow direction
  • Use pulsed-wave Doppler to measure flow patterns
  • Calculate portal vein pulsatility index

4. Renal Vein Assessment

Renal Vein Doppler Assessment

Technique

Patient Positioning

Left lateral decubitus position for right renal vein, right lateral decubitus for left renal vein.

Probe Positioning

Place the curvilinear probe in the lateral position at the level of the renal hilum. Angle slightly to optimize visualization of the renal vein.

Instructions

  • Identify the renal vein at the renal hilum
  • Apply color Doppler to confirm venous flow
  • Use pulsed-wave Doppler to assess flow pattern
  • Measure peak velocities during both respiratory phases
  • Document venous flow pattern (continuous vs. phasic)
  • Assess for presence of renal venous congestion
