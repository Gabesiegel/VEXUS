VExUS Image Acquisition Guide

Equipment and Preparation

  • Ultrasound Machine: 2D imaging, color Doppler, and pulsed-wave Doppler capabilities
  • Transducer: Phased array or curvilinear transducer
  • Additional Equipment: ECG module (recommended)
  • Patient Position: Supine, examination table at operator's waist level

1. IVC Assessment

Technique:

  • Place transducer 1-2 cm below xiphoid process
  • Measure maximal diameter 2 cm below RA-IVC junction
  • Use B-mode for accuracy

2. Hepatic Vein Assessment

Long Axis View

Assess for proper waveform patterns including S and D waves

Short Axis View

Confirm positioning and flow patterns

Subxiphoid View

Alternative approach for optimal visualization

3. Portal Vein Assessment

Subxiphoid Approach

Primary approach for PV assessment

Long Axis View

Evaluate flow patterns and pulsatility

Short Axis View

Confirm findings and assess flow characteristics

4. Intrarenal Vein Assessment

Long Axis Approach

Primary view for renal vein assessment

Short Axis View

Confirmatory view for complete assessment

VExUS Grading System

Grade Criteria
Grade 0 IVC ≤ 2 cm
Grade 1 IVC > 2 cm, one or more mildly abnormal Doppler waveforms
Grade 2 IVC > 2 cm, one severely abnormal waveform
Grade 3 IVC > 2 cm, two or more severely abnormal waveforms

Important Considerations

  • Combine with patient history and physical examination
  • VExUS does not differentiate between volume and pressure overload
  • Not a tool for volume responsiveness
  • Should not be performed in isolation
  • Primarily studied in cardiac surgery and heart failure patients
