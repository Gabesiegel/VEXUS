VExUS Image Acquisition Guide

This guide provides detailed instructions for acquiring high-quality ultrasound images for VExUS assessment.

IVC Assessment

Probe Position:

Place probe in subxiphoid or subcostal position to obtain a longitudinal view of the IVC.

Key Points:

  • Place patient in supine position
  • Use a low frequency (curvilinear) probe
  • Measure IVC diameter 2-3 cm from right atrial junction
  • Assess for inspiratory collapse (if applicable)
  • Record maximum diameter for VExUS classification

    • Key Structures:

    • Inferior Vena Cava (IVC)
    • Right atrial junction
    • Hepatic vein confluence
    • Liver

      • Special Notes:

      • Ensure measurement is taken during normal respiration
      • Document both maximum and minimum diameters if significant respiratory variation
      • Consider patient's volume status and positioning when interpreting measurements

Hepatic Vein Assessment

Long Axis View

Probe Position:

Place the probe in the right intercostal space, angled towards the right shoulder.

Key Structures:

  • Hepatic vein
  • IVC junction
  • Liver parenchyma

    • Doppler Waveforms:

    • Triphasic: Normal venous return
    • Biphasic: Mild congestion
    • Monophasic: Severe congestion

Short Axis View

Probe Position:

Curvilinear probe in the right mid-axillary line at the level of the xiphoid or slightly below, angled towards the patient's head. Probe rotated 90 degrees counterclockwise from long-axis position.

Key Structures:

  • Hepatic veins
  • Portal vein
  • IVC

Subxiphoid View

Probe Position:

Probe in subxiphoid position, angling superiorly and slightly to the patient's right.

Key Structures:

  • Left hepatic vein
  • Middle hepatic vein
  • Liver
  • Right atrium
  • IVC

Portal Vein Assessment

Long Axis View

Probe Position:

Place probe in the right intercostal space, angled slightly cephalad.

Key Structures:

  • Portal vein main trunk
  • Hepatic artery
  • Liver parenchyma

    • Doppler Waveforms:

    • Normal: Continuous flow with minimal pulsatility
    • Pulsatile: Mild congestion
    • Highly pulsatile: Severe congestion

Short Axis View

Probe Position:

Probe in mid-axillary line, below the xiphoid process, angled slightly toward patient's right shoulder, rotated 90 degrees counterclockwise from long axis view.

Special Note:

This view can be helpful for distinguishing portal vein pulsations from those originating from the nearby hepatic artery.

Subxiphoid View

Probe Position:

Probe in subxiphoid position, angling superiorly and slightly to the patient's right.

Key Structures:

  • Portal vein
  • Liver
  • IVC

Renal Vein Assessment

Long Axis View

Probe Position:

Position the probe in the right flank, posterior to the mid-axillary line.

Key Structures:

  • Renal vein
  • IVC junction
  • Kidney
  • Right renal artery

    • Doppler Waveforms:

    • Continuous: Normal venous return
    • Pulsatile: Mild congestion
    • Discontinuous/phasic reversal: Severe congestion

Short Axis View

Probe Position:

Position probe in transverse orientation at right flank, looking for the kidney in cross-section.

Key Structures:

  • Renal vein
  • Renal artery
  • Kidney hilum

    • Special Notes:

    In this view, the renal vein is typically found anterior to the renal artery.

General Acquisition Tips

  • Ensure proper patient positioning for optimal image quality
  • Adjust Doppler settings appropriately for each vessel
  • Maintain a consistent angle of insonation
  • Document both 2D images and Doppler waveforms
  • Consider patient's breathing cycle during image acquisition
  • Optimize gain and depth settings for each view
