VExUS Literature Review

A comprehensive overview of key publications and research on the Venous Excess Ultrasound (VExUS) score

VExUS Validation and Foundational Research

Quantifying systemic congestion with Point-Of-Care ultrasound: development of the venous excess ultrasound grading system
Beaubien-Souligny W, Rola P, Haycock K, Bouchard J, Lamarche Y, Spiegel R, Denault AY. Ultrasound J. 2020 Apr 9;12(1):16. doi: 10.1186/s13089-020-00163-w.
This seminal paper presents the original derivation of the VExUS grading system, establishing a robust methodology for systematic venous congestion assessment.
Exemplifies methodologically sound clinical scoring tool development, utilizing both training and validation datasets, a model for diagnostic tool development broadly applicable to various clinical domains.
Notes limitations regarding narrow inclusion criteria that may impact generalizability of findings to broader patient populations.
Demonstrates VExUS grade 3 (severe congestion) as a significant predictor of cardiorenal acute kidney injury.

Prospective Evaluation of Venous Excess Ultrasound for Estimation of Venous Congestion
Longino A, Martin K, Leyba K, Siegel G, Thai TN, Riscinti M, Douglas IS, Gill E, Burke J. Chest. 2024 Mar;165(3):590-600. doi: 10.1016/j.chest.2023.09.029.
This validation study evaluates VExUS against right heart catheterization in a diverse hospital cohort, providing strong evidence for its clinical utility.
Establishes robust correlations between VExUS scores and key hemodynamic parameters including right atrial pressure, pulmonary capillary wedge pressure, and mean pulmonary artery pressure.
Documents significant association between VExUS scoring and acute kidney injury incidence.
Demonstrates correlation between VExUS assessment and diuretic response during hospitalization, suggesting potential utility in guiding fluid management strategies.
Access Full Article
Reliability and reproducibility of the venous excess ultrasound (VExUS) score, a multi-site prospective study: validating a novel ultrasound technique for comprehensive assessment of venous congestion
Longino AA, Martin KC, Leyba KR, McCormack L, Siegel G, Sharma VM, Riscinti M, Lopez CO, Douglas IS, Gill EA. Crit Care. 2024 Jun 11;28(1):197. doi: 10.1186/s13054-024-04961-9.
This multi-site study provides critical validation of VExUS scoring reproducibility and inter-rater reliability, addressing fundamental questions about the clinical utility of ultrasound waveform interpretation.
Demonstrates high inter-rater reliability for the overall VExUS score, exceeding the reliability of its individual component measurements.
Documents improved reproducibility with electrocardiogram synchronization during assessment.

VExUS Applications in Specific Patient Populations

Combination of Inferior Vena Cava Diameter, Hepatic Venous Flow, and Portal Vein Pulsatility Index: Venous Excess Ultrasound Score (VEXUS Score) in Predicting Acute Kidney Injury in Patients with Cardiorenal Syndrome: A Prospective Cohort Study
Bhardwaj V, Vikneswaran G, Rola P, Singh GR, Thakral A, Bhasin D, Singhal A, Shah H, Gupta M, Dalal S, Gupta A. Indian Journal of Critical Care Medicine (2020): 10.5005/jp-journals-10071-23570
Documents progressive normalization of Doppler waveforms corresponding with resolution of cardiorenal acute kidney injury.
Provides evidence for serial VExUS assessments as potential markers of clinical improvement.

Assessment of venous congestion with venous excess ultrasound score in the prognosis of acute heart failure in the emergency department: a prospective study
Landi I, Guerritore L, Iannaccone A, Ricotti A, Rola P, Garrone M. Eur Heart J Open. 2024 Jul 10;4(5):oeae050. doi: 10.1093/ehjopen/oeae050.
Demonstrates association between severe congestion (grade 3) and clinically significant outcomes including mortality, hospital readmission, and acute kidney injury.
Although limited by sample size, provides foundational evidence for larger investigations to develop more nuanced risk stratification models.

VExUS in the Intensive Care Setting

Early Trajectory of Venous Excess Ultrasound Score is Associated with Clinical Outcomes of General ICU Patients
Trigkidis KK, Siempos II, Kotanidou A, Zakynthinos S, Routsi C, Kokkoris S. Shock. 2024 Mar 1;61(3):400-405. doi: 10.1097/SHK.0000000000002321.
Identifies trends in VExUS scores, rather than static measurements, as significant predictors of major adverse kidney events.
Suggests the dynamic nature of venous congestion in critical illness aligns with the proposed pathophysiological mechanisms underlying VExUS assessment.
Indicates potential limitations of point-in-time VExUS assessment for critically ill patients, where hemodynamic fluctuations may necessitate serial evaluations.
Access Full Article
Prevalence of systemic venous congestion assessed by Venous Excess Ultrasound Grading System (VExUS) and association with acute kidney injury in a general ICU cohort: a prospective multicentric study
Andrei S, Bahr PA, Nguyen M, Bouhemad B, Guinot PG. Crit Care. 2023 Jun 8;27(1):224. doi: 10.1186/s13054-023-04524-4.
Documents relatively low prevalence of VExUS-detected congestion in a general ICU cohort.
Failed to demonstrate association between VExUS scores and acute kidney injury in this heterogeneous population.
Highlights potential limitations of VExUS in unselected critically ill populations with complex, multifactorial disease processes.
